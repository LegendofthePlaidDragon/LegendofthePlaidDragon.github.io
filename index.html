Welcome to Legend of the Plaid Dragon.

Legend of the Plaid Dragon is a text-based role-playing game where players develop a character, slay beasts and level up so they can... slay bigger monsters, and eventually defeat the Plaid Dragon, who terrorizes the mystical village of Coneshire.

You can invite the LotPD bot into your Slack with the button below, or play it directly on the web.

Wait, what?

First, some history: way back in the bad old days, before everyone had high-speed internet and supercomputers in their pockets, even before generational landmarks like AOL or CompuServe really got big, people used "bulletin board systems" - BBSes - to do their interneting. It wasn't really the internet, but we didn't know any better then.

BBSes often offered what were called "door games", which were swappable external text-based games that users could play. (Remember, these were in the days when a single image could take 5-10 minutes to load on you 2400-baud modem.) They were awesome. And when I was in middle school, I was fascinated with one game in particular: Seth Robinson's "Legend of the Red Dragon" (LORD). It was, not surprisingly, one of the most popular door games of all time.

My fascination with LORD led me and a buddy, then also about 15, to build our own BBS from scratch. We built a computer, bought some software, learned some basic code and made it a thing. It was the first thing I programmed and built. I now work in enterprise software, and I credit our BBS, and LORD, for being one of the sparks for everything that came afterwards. A game literally kind of changed my life.

LORD, like the BBSes it ran on, is now long-gone, and has mostly been forgotten. So when I began experimenting with Meteor and Node development and was thinking about a project to work on, building a LORD for the modern web was what I landed on. Slack provides a perfect runtime for a text-based game, and gave me an opportunity to build my first chatbot.

This is all very much a WIP, and your comments/suggestions/etc are welcome. I don't really know what I'm doing.

Why the name Coneshire?

Because this was pretty much my wife's reaction too.
